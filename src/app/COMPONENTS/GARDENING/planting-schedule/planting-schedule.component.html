A hardiness zone is a geographically-defined zone in which a specific category of plant life is capable of growing, as defined by temperature hardiness, or ability to withstand the minimum temperatures of the zone.

Sacramento is located in zone 9B. This means that the lowest temperatures recorded from 1976 through 2005, ranged from 25 ℉ to 30 ℉.

The first Fall frost occurs around the 26th of November ; while the last Spring frost occurs around March 1st.

The following table allows you to search for a vegetable in order to find the most appropriate time to sow your seeds or plant your seedlings.

Environment:

  • Sow outdoors: Sow seeds directly into the ground outside and without cover.
  • Sow indoors: Sow seeds indoors either on a window sill, inside a greenhouse, or under lights.
  • Sow undercover: Sow seeds outdoors but under the protection of a fleece.

Transplanting of seedlings does not have a specific date tied to it. This task should be done once the seedlings reach the appropriate maturity level. This is variable based on growing conditions. Under normal conditions, this is around 4 weeks after the initial sowing. At least two inches of plant growth is advised prior to transplanting however.
