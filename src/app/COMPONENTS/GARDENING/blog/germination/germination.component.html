SEED GERMINATION


Germination of seed is entirely dependent on soil temperature. Because of this, germinating seeds indoors, even with very little to no sunlight, for roughly 5 days to a week is actually ideal.

Large seeds such as beans or beets can be sown in container cells with anywhere from two to four seeds per.

Smaller seeds such as that of celery or onion can be sown in containers without cells as they will need to be "pricked out".

Once seeds have sprouted and the germinated seeds have their cotyledons or first set of leaves (Not true leaves), they are ready to be pricked out.
