SEED GERMINATION

Germination of seed is almost entirely dependent on soil temperature. Because of this, seeds can be germinated indoors with very little to no sunlight. 5 to 7 days of warm, moist conditions, is all that is needed.

Large seeds such as beans or beets can be sown in container cells with anywhere from two to four seeds per cell.

Smaller seeds such as that of celery or onion can be sown directly in containers without cells as they will need to be "pricked out".

Once seeds have sprouted and the germinated seeds have their cotyledons, or first set of leaves (Not true leaves), they are ready to be pricked out.
